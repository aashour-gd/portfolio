## Content Type Playbooks

### CV / Resume Bullets
- **Formula:** `[Outcome verb] [specific metric/result] by [action] using [tool/method] for [context/scale]`
- Lead with impact: retention lifted, handoff reduced, markets enabled, products shipped
- Never write "Responsible for" or "Handled" — those are duty words, not achievement words
- Engineering degree and Head of UX title must appear in the summary, not just in their respective sections
- ATS keywords must be in both the Skills section AND woven into bullets
- Skills section: use labeled rows (Design Tools / Prototyping / Design Systems / UX Methods / AI-Assisted Design / Visual & Motion / Cross-Functional) — not a comma-soup paragraph

### LinkedIn Headline
- Format: `[Role] · [Strongest technical signal] · [Market intent/availability]`
- Current: *"Senior Product Designer · Design Systems & Mobile UX · Open to opportunities in Saudi, Gulf & Remote Europe"*
- Only change if target market or positioning shifts

### LinkedIn About Section
- Opens with a big-picture statement about what Ahmed believes or how he thinks (not a career summary)
- Flows into what he does and how he's different (engineering background is the hook)
- Ends with a clear CTA for hiring managers
- Reads like a human wrote it, not a PR team

### LinkedIn Posts
**Structure:** Hook → Story/Tension → Insight → Invitation
**Hook types that work:**
- A surprising constraint he faced ("We had 2 weeks to build a design system. From scratch.")
- A belief he holds ("Design systems aren't about components. They're about decisions.")
- A story moment ("The PM said: just make it look good. I said: let me show you something first.")

**Post rules:**
- 150–400 words sweet spot
- Line breaks between every 1–3 sentences (LinkedIn formatting)
- End with a question or invitation to respond
- No hashtag spam — 2–3 max, relevant only
- Never post about being "open to work" — post about the *work*
- Story-driven posts get 5–10x more recruiter inbound than static updates

### Portfolio Case Studies
**Strict 6-part structure:**
**Problem → Role → Hard Decision → What We Built → Result → Learning**

Each section 2–5 sentences. The Hard Decision is the most important — it's where Ahmed's judgment shows. Never skip or make it vague.

**AFIF specifics:** The ethical design decision (transparency over secrecy in child safety UX) is the differentiating angle. Name the tension. Defend the choice. This is not a feature decision — it's a values decision, and that's what makes the case study stand out.

### Cover Letters / Outreach Messages
- Open with a specific observation about the company or role (not "I've always admired...")
- Connect one of Ahmed's real experiences to their specific problem
- Engineering background = natural conversation opener for technical product companies
- End with a low-friction ask (a conversation, not "please review my application")
- Under 200 words for cold outreach; under 350 for formal cover letters

### Bio (Short form, 50–80 words)
Ahmed is a Senior Product Designer with 10+ years of experience across SaaS, mobile, and e-commerce. Trained as an Electronics & Communications Engineer, he approaches design like a systems problem — starting with constraints, not aesthetics. He's built design systems, led UX teams, and shipped products across 3 markets. Currently Head of UX at Intouch, open to senior roles in the Gulf region and remote Europe.

## Audience Targeting Guide

When the audience is **Gulf/KSA hiring managers** → Lead with cross-market experience, mobile-first work, engineering credibility, and any GCC-adjacent market context. Mention Vision 2030-relevant sectors (fintech, edtech, e-commerce) if applicable.

When the audience is **European remote companies** → Lead with design systems depth, autonomous working style, measurable metrics, and accessibility awareness.

When the audience is **US growth-stage startups / FAANG** → Lead with 0→1 proof (AFIF, Intouch), AI fluency signals, and engineering background for technical credibility.

When the audience is **unknown** → Default to growth-stage startup hiring manager. Engineering background + design systems + shipped products = the universal senior signal.

When the content is **LinkedIn** → Write for all three personas simultaneously. Founders skim hooks. Recruiters scan metrics. Hiring managers read case studies.

When the content is **a case study** → Write for the hiring manager who will read it fully. Depth and judgment matter more than brevity here.

## Hard Rules

**Never write:**
- "I am passionate about UX/design"
- "I am excited/thrilled/honored to share"
- "Results-driven" or "detail-oriented" as opening descriptors
- "Pixel-perfect" as a standalone claim
- "I helped the team" (vague contribution framing)
- "Responsible for" or "Handled" (duty-listing)
- "Worked alongside" without specifying the outcome of that collaboration
- Soft skills in a skills list (creativity, attention to detail) — demonstrate them through bullets instead

**Always write:**
- Specific actions Ahmed personally took
- The business or human problem behind the design decision
- At least one moment of real judgment or trade-off
- A result — even if it's "we learned X" rather than a metric
- Named tools and methods (Figma Variables, not just "Figma")
